Mirr exists to solve one specific, frustrating problem: buying clothes online means guessing. We think customers deserve better than guessing, and brands deserve fewer returns.

THE PROBLEM

Online shopping broke one thing that physical stores always got right.

Walk into a store and you can hold a garment up, try it on, and know in seconds whether it fits and whether it suits you. Buy online and you're left staring at a size chart, hoping a "Medium" from one brand means the same thing as a "Medium" from another. It usually doesn't.

The result is an industry-wide problem: online fashion return rates regularly sit between 20-40%, driven almost entirely by sizing and fit uncertainty, not product quality. Every one of those returns costs a brand money, costs the planet in shipping emissions, and costs the customer the disappointment of an item that didn't work out.

WHAT WE BUILT

A marketplace where you see yourself in the product before you buy it.

Mirr combines two things that have always existed separately in online retail: virtual try-on, so you can see roughly how a garment looks on you, and dimension-based fit scoring, so you know precisely how it fits against your actual body measurements — not just a generic size label.

We didn't want to build another size-chart calculator, and we didn't want to build a filter that just slaps a garment over a photo for a laugh. We wanted both pieces of the puzzle working together, because seeing and knowing it fits are different problems that both need solving before someone clicks "buy" with confidence.

Our goal was never to build flashy technology. It was to stop people from ordering three sizes of the same shirt just to find out which one fits.
WHY A MARKETPLACE

We chose to own the full experience, not rent it out.

Early on we considered building a plugin that brands could bolt onto their own websites. We decided against it. A plugin means every brand's customer has a different, inconsistent experience, and Mirr has no control over the quality of what customers actually see.

Instead, Mirr is a destination — a marketplace where customers come specifically because they want the try-on and fit-check experience, and brands list their products into an environment built entirely around solving the fit problem. One consistent experience, one clear story, full control over quality.

WHERE WE'RE HEADED

This is the beginning, not the finished product.

Mirr launched with front-facing virtual try-on and dimension-based fit scoring — already ahead of what most fashion retailers offer today. Multi-angle try-on, deeper size guidance, and tools that help brands understand exactly why a return happened are all part of where we're going next.

We're building this with our founding brands, not for them from a distance. Every brand that lists in these early days shapes what Mirr becomes.
